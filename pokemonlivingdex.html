Catching every pokemon is a complicated task

Catching them all. It has been the dream pokemon players since Pokemon Red and Blue first came out 20 years ago. It was a much simpler goal back when there were only a few games and 151 pokemon. However, with over 806 pokemon to catch spread out across 7 generations, and 25 core games, the journey has become not only harder, but far more complicated. Because each there are so many games, each with its own unique set of pokemon. It can be difficult to tell just what path, and which games you should buy to help complete your ultimate pokemon quest. Do you need to buy all 25, or would just a few of the games be enough to complete your collection?

Lets say you have the following games:

  1. Pokemon Ultra Moon
  2. Pokemon Alpha Saphire
  3. Pokemon Yellow: Special Pickachu edition (3DS)
  4. Pokemon Saphire

Is this enough games to capture all 806 pokemon? What if you had a friend with Pokemon Ultra Sun to trade you all the missing pokemon from Pokemon Ultra Moon? If you can't catch them all in just those games, how many can you catch? What if you received some event pokemon that filled in some of the gaps how many would you need then? The question is difficult to answer.
